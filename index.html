    SCRATCH PROGRAMMING

    Scratch is a visual programming language that allows users to create interactive stories, games, and animations without any prior programming experience. The Scratch platform is designed to be fun, easy to use, and accessible to a wide range of ages and skill levels. The drag-and-drop interface allows users to create their own programs by combining pre-built blocks of code, without having to write any code themselves.

    SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

    Software engineering is the application of engineering principles to the design and development of software. It involves the use of techniques like requirement analysis, design, implementation, testing, and maintenance to create software that is reliable, secure, efficient, and easy to use. It also involves the use of best practices, such as following coding standards and using version control, to ensure the quality of the software.

    IOT & ROBOTICS

    IOT (Internet of Things) is a network of physical objects that are connected to the internet and can collect and share data. These objects can be anything from sensors and actuators to vehicles and appliances. IOT is often used in conjunction with artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) to allow these objects to make decisions and take actions based on the data they collect. For example, a self-driving car uses sensors to collect data about its surroundings and uses that data to navigate safely. Robotics is the field of technology that deals with the design, construction, and operation of robots.

    About

    About

    Bridging the Gap to Technological enhancements, reducing poverty and unemployment and creating empowerment to the youths.

    Overview

    Nigeria, the most populous black nation, is home to around 200 million people with an annual population growth of 2.61 percent. Currently, over 33.6 million (16.8 percent) Nigerians are regarded as youths (aged between 15 and 35). However, according to statistics, about 43.69 percent of Nigeria’s total population were aged 0 to 14 in 2019. This means that in the near future, the youth will increasingly form the bulk of the country’s population as children grow older into young adults. According to the latest Labour Force report of the National Bureau of Statistics, unemployment among young Nigerians (15- 34 years) is the highest in the country, with 21.72 million or 42.5 percent of the 29.94 young Nigerians in the labour force unemployed, while the national unemployment rate stood at 33.3 percent as at December 2020. This has created discouraged, angry and restless youths who look at a future that does not give them hope.

    Mission Objective

    As part of our Corporate Social Responsibility, and taking advantage of the large pool of young and unemployed youths in Nigeria, we would like to set up internet libraries / software development academies that would train teenagers and youth in the art of software engineering, machine learning, Blockchain technology and Artificial Intelligence. We believe these technologies will be goal drivers for change bringing in lasting development to our local economy, country, world and most importantly to the participant. This will also largely be possible because of the low barrier to entry in the tech ecosystem. Currently, we train young teenagers the art of software development with emphasis on Web development, this most times serves as a launching pad for easy entry for participants. Expansion to further technologies like Machine learning, Artificial Intelligence, Mobile App development and Blockchain will follow.

    Approach

    We would start at our locality and reach out to a minimum set of 50 teenagers and youth per enrollment cycle. From the enrollment cycle to training completion will last 3 months. This number of enrollees will increase as we have capacity. We would orientate and sensitive them regarding these (Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, Mobile App development, Machine Learning, Web development) and the major roles they play in crafting our collective future. Attendees must have a little practical knowledge of using computers to be eligible. Training is free. Certificates will be issued at the end of the training. We would leverage other resources in following through with learning mediums like Coursera, Udemy and PluralSight.
